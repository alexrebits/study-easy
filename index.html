Flashcards are a classic study method primarily used for memorization and active recall.

The Pomodoro Technique is a method of time management that promotes focused work intervals. It involves 25 minutes of uninterupted work followed by a 5 minute break, also known as a "Pomodoro." 4 of these Pomodoro cycles is followe by a 15-30 minute break.

The Feynman Technique is a 4-step learning method originally used by the American quantum physicist Richard Feynman. This process prioritizes understanding over rote memorization and is made up of 4 steps:

  • Perform initial learning
  • Pretend to explain this concept to a child
  • Identify gaps in your understanding
  • Review, revise, repeat
